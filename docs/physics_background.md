# Physics Background

## Higgs Boson Production at the LHC

The Higgs boson is produced at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) primarily through:
- **Gluon-gluon fusion (ggF)**: Dominant production mode
- **Vector boson fusion (VBF)**: Second most common
- **Associated production**: With W/Z bosons or top quarks

## H→bb̄ Decay Channel

The Higgs boson decays to b-quark pairs (H→bb̄) with a branching ratio of ~58%. This is the dominant decay mode but challenging due to:
- Large QCD background
- b-jet identification requirements
- Mass resolution limitations

## Signal vs Background

**Signal Events**: Higgs boson production and decay to b-quark pairs
**Background Events**: QCD multijet production, W/Z+jets, top quark production

## Key Discriminating Variables

1. **m_bb_paper**: Dijet invariant mass - peaks at Higgs mass (~125 GeV)
2. **B-tagging**: Identifies b-jets from Higgs decay
3. **Mass matching**: Validates reconstruction quality
4. **Missing energy**: Indicates neutrinos or other invisible particles
5. **Rapidity gaps**: Characteristic of VBF production

## Traditional Cut-Based Analysis

Traditional physics cuts (De Roeck et al. 2002):
- B-tagging > 0.3
- Jet pT > 30 GeV
- Result: 0 events pass (complete failure)
